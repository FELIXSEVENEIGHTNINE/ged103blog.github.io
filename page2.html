Rizal Park

Rizal Park, still widely known as ‘Luneta Park‘ (which was its name until the 1950s), spreads out over 60 hectares of open lawns, gardens, ponds, paved walks, and wooded areas. Rizal Park houses monuments to the pantheon of famous Filipino heroes. As the well-known spot where the hero José Rizal was executed, the park holds great historical significance.

I had a good time being here, the park was a sight, especially the greenery since I like pictures of nature. Let me think outside of the box and say that this shows the resilency of the Filipinos. The way that Rizal's place where he was exiled is still in a good condition could mean that Filipinos are resilient, and still to this day. Resilient in defending the country and resilient in today's issues like natural disasters.
