Rizal Park

Rizal Park, still widely known as ‘Luneta Park‘ (which was its name until the 1950s), spreads out over 60 hectares of open lawns, gardens, ponds, paved walks, and wooded areas. Rizal Park houses monuments to the pantheon of famous Filipino heroes. As the well-known spot where the hero José Rizal was executed, the park holds great historical significance.

You've missed a good part of your childhood if you've not been here. I remember being here (before the photobomb building was built) and would use my bicycle around the park. A park dedicated to the execution place of a national hero is really interesting and fun. It reminds Filipinos of what the national hero did for the Philippines, even though I think he was caught in the crossfire.
